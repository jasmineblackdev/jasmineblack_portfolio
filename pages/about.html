About

I Design Products That Actually Get Built

Most UX portfolios show pretty mockups that never shipped. Mine shows products I designed, coded, and deployed—because I spent 5+ years learning that beautiful designs mean nothing if developers can't build them.

I'm a product designer based in Charlotte, NC, with hands in both Figma and code. I spent the first half of my career in graphic design and digital marketing, which taught me one thing: design doesn't matter if it doesn't convert. Now I apply that same ruthless focus on outcomes to UX.

How I Got Here

I started as a graphic designer in 2019, creating marketing assets and websites for healthcare and e-commerce clients. But I kept running into the same problem: beautiful designs that users didn't understand, or developers couldn't build.

So I learned to code. HTML, CSS, JavaScript, React—not to become a developer, but to design smarter. To understand constraints. To speak the language of the people building my designs. I earned my Google UX Design Certificate in 2022, then immediately applied it to real client work at Response Mine Interactive and Hype.

Now I'm finishing my B.S. in Software Engineering at WGU (graduating 2027) while working as a UI/UX Designer. I design products I can also build, which means my designs are grounded in technical reality—not fantasy.

What I Bring to Your Team

Designer who codes: I prototype in HTML/CSS, not just Figma. Developers love working with me.
Product thinking: Every design decision ties back to business goals and user outcomes.
Fast execution: I've shipped projects in 2-week sprints at agencies. I know how to move quickly without sacrificing quality.
GitHub-backed work: All my projects include repositories. You can see my code, not just mockups.
Accessibility-first: WCAG compliant by default. Inclusive design isn't optional.
Cross-functional collaboration: 5+ years working with developers, marketers, and stakeholders across healthcare, fintech, and e-commerce.
